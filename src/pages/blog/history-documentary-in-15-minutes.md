### The Tutorial: From Blank Page to History Channel

#### Step 1: The "Mega-Prompt"
Don't write a lazy prompt. To get a documentary style, you need to be specific about the *tone*.
* **Open InVideo AI** and select "YouTube Explainer."
* **Paste this Prompt:**
    > *"Create a 8-minute documentary about 'The Unsolved Mystery of the Mary Celeste.' Use a deep, serious British male voice. Use a mix of realistic stock footage, archival paper textures, and dramatic re-enactments. The pacing should be slow and suspenseful. Start with a hook about the ship being found drifting."*

#### Step 2: The Audience Selection
InVideo will ask who this is for.
* **Select:** "History Buffs" or "True Crime Fans."
* **Look & Feel:** Choose "Dark" or "Cinematic."

#### Step 3: The Generation (Magic Moment)
Hit "Generate." In about 3 minutes, InVideo will write the script, record the voiceover, and—most importantly—**search its premium library (Storyblocks/Shutterstock)** for historical clips that match every sentence.

#### Step 4: The "Director's Cut"
Watch the preview. It will be 90% perfect, but you might want to change a clip.
* **Magic Command:** Instead of dragging clips, just type in the edit box: *"Change the clip at 0:45 to a stormy ocean view."*
* The AI will swap it instantly.

#### Step 5: Export
Download in 1080p (or 4K). You now have a video that looks like it cost $500 to produce.
